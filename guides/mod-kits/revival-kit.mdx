### Authentication

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Xbox One/Series (Rock Band 4)">
    To play Rock Band 4 on your Xbox with a MiniHost Adapter, you will have to authenticate the MiniHost with a licensed Xbox One or Xbox Series controller.

    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Turn on the Xbox One/Series, then using your Xbox controller, load into Rock Band 4.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Disconnect the Xbox controller from your Xbox One/Series. (Pop out batteries)
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Connect the licensed Xbox controller into the female USB-A port on the V3 Adapter.

        <Tip>
          - **Xbox One – models 1537, 1697, and 1698**
          - **Xbox One S – models 1708 and 1797**
          - **Xbox Series S/X – model 1914**
        </Tip>

        <Frame>
          <img
            src="/images/MiniHost-Adapter/MiniHostAuthPort.png"
            alt="Mini Host Auth Port"
            title="Mini Host Auth Port"
            style={{ width:"54%" }}
          />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Plug the adapter into the console with the gamepad plugged in.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Wait a moment, then press start on the gamepad to verify the connection is made. On the “Join” screen, a guitar player should appear.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Unplug the licensed Xbox controller from your device.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Plug in your guitar, if applicable.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Play the game!
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    If your controller is connected under the profile you want to use for the guitar, you’ll need to “drop out” the controller before assigning that profile to the guitar.

    (image)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Fortnite Festival

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="PC, Playstation 4/5, Xbox One/Series - Keyboard Mode" iconType="regular">
    **NOTE: EPIC HAS CHANGED A SETTING IN FORTNITE THAT HIDES PRO MODES NORMALLY. YOU WILL WANT TO ADJUST THIS TO ALWAYS HAVE PRO MODES SHOWING. TO FIX THIS:**

    **GO TO Settings \> GAME \> scroll to bottom \> change “Always Display Pro Parts” to ON.**

    <Frame>
      <img
        src="/images/fortnitefestivalsettings.png"
        alt="Fortnitefestivalsettings"
        title="Fortnitefestivalsettings"
        className="mx-auto"
        style={{ width:"55%" }}
      />
    </Frame>

    <Tip>
      ## Move the MiniHost adapter's toggle switch to "K".
    </Tip>

    This will put your guitar into keyboard mode, where your inputs will be assigned to the default Fortnite Festival Pro Lead Keyboard settings:

    - 1-5 for frets
    - Right Ctrl & Right Shift for strum
    - Forward Slash (/) for whammy
    - Page Down for overdrive

    You will know it's working if your instrument changes when you press the frets in the Festival Main Stage Lobby.

    _Note that you will have to control your menus/character movement with keyboard & mouse/other controller, but once you load into the game from the Festival Main Stage, the guitar will function as intended!_\
    **Have fun!**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Nintendo Switch">
    **NOTE: You will have to switch between two modes while navigating and playing, so be ready to do that often.**

    **MODE SWITCH BUTTON COMBO:**

    <Warning>
      # <u>YELLOW \+ START</u>

      _(press and hold yellow first, then tap start once)_
    </Warning>

    <Steps>
      <Step>
        **You want your MiniHost Adapter to be on the Default (GH Guitar) preset.** If you have changed yours to be something different, please download the latest [RCM Programming Tool](https://retrocultmods.com/programming-tool/) and factory reset it back to Default. You will have to calibrate your whammy (and tilt if needed).
      </Step>
      <Step>
        **Turn on your Nintendo Switch and plug your adapter/guitar in!** It should be able to navigate the NS menu immediately. If not, unplug and plug in the adapter/guitar again.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Once you have confirmed that your adapter/guitar is working on the Switch, **open Fortnite Festival.**
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Once the game is open, **you should be able to navigate the game with the guitar**, at least somewhat.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Open your game settings, then switch to the GAME tab. Scroll to the bottom and **switch the “ALWAYS DISPLAY PRO PARTS” to ON.**

        <Frame>
          <img
            src="/images/fortnitefestivalsettings.png"
            alt="Fortnitefestivalsettings"
            title="Fortnitefestivalsettings"
            style={{ width:"44%" }}
          />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Load into Fortnite Festival Main Stage, then select your songs. **Once you ready up, HOLD YELLOW then tap START**. Your guitar should then function to for play!
      </Step>
      <Step>
        After your song is over, do the **YELLOW\+START combo again to be able to navigate**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
