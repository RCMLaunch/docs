## Soldering

**INFO YOU NEED TO KNOW:**

### Temperature

**Soldering Iron temperature:** 600°F or 315°C (Leaded Solder)\
675**°**F or 360**°**C (Lead Free Solder). I personally use the hotter temperature with leaded solder.

**Cold Joints** can appear as a bubble or a more matte finish on your solder joint. If this happens, hold the iron and melt the solder again, but hold your iron there briefly so the heat transfers to the pins properly.

### Tinning Your Tip

**Tinning Your Iron’s Tip** is a good habit when beginning a solder job. What you do is:

1. Turn on your iron and set your it to the proper soldering temperature if it isn’t already set, allow it to reach that temperature.
2. Put a little bit of solder on the tip of your iron, just enough to coat the part you solder with.
3. Clean the solder off of it using a damp sponge or brass wool. You will see that the tip is shiny. That means it’s tinned!

### Universal Fretboard

<Info>
  Components:

  - Universal Fretboard
  - Kailh Choc Low Profile Red switches
  - 90-degree Pin Headers
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Tin your soldering tip and clean it.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Insert the Kailh Choc Low Profile Red Switches into the fretboard, making sure that you put it in the correct way like shown below.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Bend the thinner leg closest to the square solder pad down so it has contact or is very close to the solder pad.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Put a little bit of flux on each pad, then solder the leg to the pad. Repeat this for all 5 switches.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Once you've soldered all 5 legs, insert the 90-degree Pin Headers into the circular solder pads like shown below and orient them in a way where the headers touch the other leg of the switch like shown below.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Add a dab of flux to the spot where the two pieces of metal touch to hold the 90-degree Pin Header in place.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    _This part is tricky._

    You must solder the 90 degree header to the leg of the switch. Apply heat to the area you added flux to, then apply solder. You want to make sure they are bound together with solder like shown below.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Once you've verified that all five headers have been soldered to their respective switch legs, flip the entire fretboard over. You must now connect the 90-degree Pin Headers to the fretboard. The circular pads around the headers are what must be soldered to, shown below.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Apply a small amount of flux, then carefully solder the pin header to the fretboard, being mindful to not touch the switch with soldering iron.
  </Step>
  <Step>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### RGB Fretboard

<Info>
  Components:

  - RGB Fretboard
  - 5x Kailh Choc Low Profile Red Switches
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Insert the Kailh Choc Low Profile Red Switches into the fretboard, they will only go one way. Start with the two on the far ends.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Flip the board over and push the PCB down to ensure that the switches are flush against it. Doing this on a hard surface will help.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Tin your soldering tip and clean it.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Solder the switch pins to the fretboard.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Repeat the process until you’ve soldered all five switches to the fretboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setup

### Authentication

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Xbox One/Series (Rock Band 4)">
    To play Rock Band 4 on your Xbox with a MiniHost Adapter, you will have to authenticate the MiniHost with a licensed Xbox One or Xbox Series controller.

    <Steps>
      <Step>
        Turn on the Xbox One/Series, then using your Xbox controller, load into Rock Band 4.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Disconnect the Xbox controller from your Xbox One/Series. (Pop out batteries)
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Connect the licensed Xbox controller into the female USB-A port on the V3 Adapter.

        <Tip>
          - **Xbox One – models 1537, 1697, and 1698**
          - **Xbox One S – models 1708 and 1797**
          - **Xbox Series S/X – model 1914**
        </Tip>

        <Frame>
          <img
            src="/images/MiniHost-Adapter/MiniHostAuthPort.png"
            alt="Mini Host Auth Port"
            title="Mini Host Auth Port"
            style={{ width:"54%" }}
          />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Plug the adapter into the console with the gamepad plugged in.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Wait a moment, then press start on the gamepad to verify the connection is made. On the “Join” screen, a guitar player should appear.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Unplug the licensed Xbox controller from your device.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Plug in your guitar, if applicable.
      </Step>
      <Step>
        Play the game!
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    If your controller is connected under the profile you want to use for the guitar, you’ll need to “drop out” the controller before assigning that profile to the guitar.

    <Tip>
      ## Move the MiniHost adapter's toggle switch to "K".
    </Tip>

    This will put your guitar into keyboard mode, where your inputs will be assigned to the default Fortnite Festival Pro Lead Keyboard settings:

    - 1-5 for frets
    - Right Ctrl & Right Shift for strum
    - Forward Slash (/) for whammy
    - Page Down for overdrive

    You will know it's working if your instrument changes when you press the frets in the Festival Main Stage Lobby.

    _Note that you will have to control your menus/character movement with keyboard & mouse/other controller, but once you load into the game from the Festival Main Stage, the guitar will function as intended!_\
    **Have fun!**
