The RetroCultMods DIY Solder Kit contains everything you need to convert your official Red Octane Guitar Hero controller into a modded XInput guitar controller has nearly universal platform compatibility.

<Danger>
  **_<u>This kit REQUIRES both soldering equipment and knowledge to install!</u>_**
</Danger>

[If you do not know how to solder, the Revival kits is a much better option!](https://shop.retrocultmods.com/products/revival-kit-for-guitar-hero-controllers-by-retrocultmods)

- If using the Standard Fretboard, Xplorer models other than 95065 require [3D printed frets](https://www.etsy.com/listing/1503752173/3d-printed-frets-for-guitar-hero).

- Third party guitars have not been officially tested, but feel free to give them a go!

## Soldering Tips

**<u>INFO YOU NEED TO KNOW:</u>**

### Temperature

**Soldering Iron temperature:**\
600°F or 315°C (Leaded Solder)\
675\*\*°**F or 360**°\*\*C (Lead Free Solder).

I personally use the hotter temperature with leaded solder.

**Cold Joints** can appear as a bubble or a more matte finish on your solder joint. If this happens, hold the iron and melt the solder again, but hold your iron there briefly so the heat transfers to the pins properly. If you've applied too much solder, use your solder wick.

<Accordion title="How to use Solder Wick">
  To use solder wick,  place the end of the wick over the solder you want to remove and then place your heated solder tip over it. The braid heats up and will suck up the solder. You can tell when the wick begins to pull the solder, as the wick will change color.
</Accordion>

### Tinning Your Tip

**Tinning Your Iron’s Tip** is a good habit when beginning a solder job. What you do is:

1. Turn on your iron and set your it to the proper soldering temperature if it isn’t already set, allow it to reach that temperature.
2. Put a little bit of solder on the tip of your iron, just enough to coat the part you solder with.
3. Clean the solder off of it using a damp sponge or brass wool. You will see that the tip is shiny. That means it’s tinned!

## Build

### Universal Fretboard

<Badge color="green" stroke>Standard DIY Kit</Badge>

<Accordion title="Universal Fretboard Soldering Guide">
  <Info>
    Tools used:

    - Soldering Gear
    - Flush Cutters

    Components:

    - Universal Fretboard
    - Kailh Choc Low Profile Red switches
    - 90-degree Pin Headers
  </Info>

  <Steps>
    <Step>
      Insert the Kailh Choc Low Profile Red Switches into the fretboard, making sure that you put it in the correct way like shown below.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Bend the thinner leg closest to the square solder pad down so it has contact or is very close to the solder pad.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Put a little bit of flux on each pad, then solder the leg to the pad. Repeat this for all 5 switches.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Tin your soldering tip and clean it with the brass wool.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Once you've soldered all 5 legs, insert the 90-degree Pin Headers into the circular solder pads like shown below and orient them in a way where the headers touch the other leg of the switch like shown below.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Add a dab of flux to the spot where the two pieces of metal touch to hold the 90-degree Pin Header in place.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      _This part is tricky._

      You must solder the 90 degree header to the leg of the switch. Apply heat to the area you added flux to, then apply solder. You want to make sure they are bound together with solder like shown below.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Once you've verified that all five headers have been soldered to their respective switch legs, flip the entire fretboard over. You must now connect the 90-degree Pin Headers to the fretboard. The circular pads around the headers are what must be soldered to, shown below.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Apply a small amount of flux, then carefully solder the pin header to the fretboard, being mindful to not touch the switch with soldering iron.
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Using flush cutters, cut the excess metal of the header, leaving just the "mountain" shape of soldered metal, like shown below.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Accordion>

    </Step>
    <Step>
      Solder the switch pins to the fretboard.

<Accordion title="Fretboard to Strumboard Soldering Guide">
  <Info>
    Tools used:

    - Wire Strippers
    - Flush Cutters
    - Soldering Gear

    Components:

    - Assembled Fretboard
    - Assembled Zeroboard/Strumboard
    - 6-pin 12 inch wire (2x if RGB fretboard kit)
  </Info>

  <Steps>
    <Step>
      Strip both ends of one set of wires by approximately 1cm. More is okay, you'll just have to trim it after
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Separate the wires by about an inch and a half. 
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Observe both the Zeroboard and Fretboard pins, you will see the following pins:
    </Step>
    <Step>
      Make a mental gameplan of how you'll approach the soldering. You can either solder all on one side at a time, or back and forth per wire.
    </Step>

      Twist the ends of the wires so that there's no loose strands of copper, then insert them one by one into each fret color socket and ground sockets like shown below.

    </Step>

      Flip both boards over then use your flush cutters to snip off any loose wires.
    </Step>
    <Step>

    </Step>
