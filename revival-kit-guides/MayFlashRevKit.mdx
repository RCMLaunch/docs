# Introduction

This is an instructional guide for the RetroCultMods Revival Kit for the PlayStation 2 MayFlash guitar. This build can be completed in under an hour and a half, but can take longer if you are thorough with the cleaning process. If you run into any issues during the build or have questions, [click here!](https://retrocultmods.freshdesk.com/support/tickets/new)

## Parts Included

- Revival Kit Zeroboard (Strumboard/PCB)
- Revival Kit Fretboard
- 5x 3D Printed Translucent Frets
- 3D Printed Kit Hardware
- Replacement Start/Select board for MayFlash
- TwinTail USB-C and Authentication Cable
- New Whammy Assembly **FOR MAYFLASH\*\*\***
- Switch Puller
- Color Coded Wires
- 10 foot nylon braided USB-A to USB-C cable
- Tube of lubricant

<Accordion title="Images of all parts">
  Revival Kit Zeroboard
</Accordion>

## Required Tools (not included)

- [Screwdriver with PH1, PH2, T10, and T6(sometimes) bits](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0CCTNX9GY)
- [Flush Cutters (optional)](https://www.amazon.com/IGAN-P6-Precision-Clippers-Spring-loaded-Handmade/dp/B087P191LP)

## 1. Opening the Guitar

Flip the controller over so the **Red Octane** sticker on the back of the body is visible.

Ensure that **all** screws are removed, and open the shell.\
\
Once the shell begins to open, slowly move down the rest of the shell until it pops open completely.\
Repeat this process for the neck, using the opening at the neck connector and working towards the headstock.

If the shell seems to gets stuck, **DO NOT FORCE IT**. Check to make sure you have removed **all** of the screws before continuing to pry the shell.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Gather the Revival Kit Fretboard, Translucent Frets, 3 White Wires, and 2 Long Screws.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Connect one end of each of the White Wires into the RGB, FRET1, and FRET2 connectors on the Fretboard. Connect the other end of each White Wire to the corresponding RGB, FRET1, and FRET2 connectors on the Strumboard. I recommend doing one wire at a time to prevent mixing them up.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Remove the stock silicone pad and frets if they are still in the fret slots. Wipe the inside of the fret slots clean if they are dirty. Then apply a small amount of lubricant to your finger or a qtip and lightly coat the inside of the fret slots with lubricant. Make sure to wipe away any excess lubricant that my have spilled to the front of the neck shell!

### Whammy

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open RCM Programming Tool if you do not already have it open
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Scroll to the Whammy section. Click on it to open the dropdown menu.

    <Frame>
      ![whammy calibration](images/Programmer/whammy calibration.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click the Calibrate button.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Leave the whammy in it's resting position (as in don't press it down). Click Next.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Press the whammy down into its lowest position until it comes to a stopping point. Hold it there and click Next. You can release the whammy bar now.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Now you're setting the Deadzone. Push the whammy bar down ever so slightly to observe the value moving around. You want it to have a little bit of force required before the whammy begins activating. Once you've found the sweet spot for your guitar, hold it there and click Next.

    <Frame>
      ![whammy deadzone](images/Programmer/whammy deadzone.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step>
    You're done calibrating! You'll see the Calibrated Value go up and down and you press your whammy. What you want is for the Calibrated Value to not display anything when you're not touching the whammy bar.
  </Step>
</Steps>
